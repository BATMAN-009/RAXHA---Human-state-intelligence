# Notebook A — Architecture Assumptions Disproven by Reality

> Every time implementation proves an assumption wrong, one entry. This is how the documents stay honest — and how RFCs are earned (four-trigger rule: **Reality**). An empty notebook after months of building would itself be suspicious.

| # | Assumption (doc §) | What happened | Why it failed | Resulting RFC |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A-01 (2026-07-15) | A dual-clock check (`monotonic OR wall`) reliably decides deadline expiry across a reboot (ADR-105 / D11 / SRS-504, as first coded in `EscalationFSM.isExpired`) | AUDIT-002 proved two failures: (1) a **forward wall-clock jump** (NTP/DST) fired a live countdown *early* → false family alert (H-02); (2) a **dead-RTC reboot** (wall resets to 1970, monotonic resets to ~0) made the persisted deadline pass *neither* leg, so a countdown that expired while the device was dead became **unexpirable for days** — silent missed emergency (H-01). | Monotonic clocks from different boot sessions are **fundamentally incomparable**, and the wall clock is unreliable (settable, dead-RTC). No clock-only heuristic can distinguish "deadline is later this same boot" from "deadline is from a previous boot." | **RFC-008** — persist a **boot-session identifier** in the journal so recovery can tell same-boot from cross-boot and compute remaining time correctly. Interim (shipped): `isExpired` is monotonic-only (same-boot only); `recover()` of any timed state **fails toward alerting** (safety over false-alarm cost); the false-alarm-on-benign-restart cost is the debt RFC-008 removes. |
