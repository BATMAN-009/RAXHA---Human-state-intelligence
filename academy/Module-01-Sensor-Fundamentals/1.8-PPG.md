# Chapter 1.8 — PPG (Photoplethysmography)

> **Why this chapter matters most so far.** Every sensor until now measured *motion* — what the body did. The physician on our review panel has objected twice: motion sensors miss the syncope patient who crumples softly, and "absence of rotation must never mean absence of emergency." PPG is the first answer. It measures *physiology* — whether blood is still pulsing and how fast — which is the difference between "the watch moved oddly" and "this person may be in cardiac trouble." It is also the single hardest consumer biosignal to get right, because the same wrist that carries it is constantly moving.

---

## 1. Definition

**Photoplethysmography** = "photo" (light) + "plethysmography" (measuring volume changes). A PPG sensor shines light into the skin and measures how much returns; with each heartbeat, arterial blood volume in the illuminated tissue rises and falls, absorbing more or less light. The returned-light waveform is the **PPG signal**, and its most-used derivative is **heart rate** (the pulse repetition rate).

What PPG is and is not:
- It is an **optical, indirect, peripheral** measure of the cardiac cycle — it sees the *mechanical* pulse of blood at the wrist, not the heart's *electrical* activity (that's ECG, Chapter 1.9 — the distinction is load-bearing and returns constantly).
- It yields, in rough order of reliability: heart rate (★★★★★), heart-rate variability (★★★★, motion-permitting), respiration (★★★, derived), blood-oxygen SpO₂ (★★★, multi-wavelength), and — aspirationally — blood pressure (★★, research). Each further claim rests on more assumptions; the confidence ladder is steep and you must always know which rung you're on.

## 2. History

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1937 | **Alrick Hertzman** coins "photoplethysmography," measures blood-volume changes optically | The principle established |
| 1970s–80s | **Pulse oximetry** (Takuo Aoyagi, Nihon Kohden) — two-wavelength PPG for SpO₂ | The clinical killer app; the fingertip oximeter becomes ubiquitous in hospitals |
| 1990s–2000s | Fingertip/earlobe HR monitors | PPG leaves the OR |
| **2014–15** | **Apple Watch (2015), Fitbit Charge HR** put green-LED wrist PPG on millions of wrists | The wrist era — and the motion-artifact war begins in earnest |
| 2018→ | Multi-wavelength arrays, SpO₂ on consumer watches (Apple Watch Series 6, 2020), PPG-based AFib screening | PPG becomes a screening instrument, drawing FDA attention |
| 2020s | Dozens of green/red/IR LEDs + multiple photodiodes; deep-learning motion rejection; PPG-derived respiration and sleep staging | The sensor is now an array + a signal-processing problem more than a photodiode |

**Current state:** wrist PPG for resting/steady-state HR is mature and trusted; everything beyond it degrades under motion and skin-tone variability (§12, §14 — an equity issue, not a footnote). **Trend:** sensor fusion with the accelerometer *inside* the PPG pipeline (the accel is not just a companion sensor — it is the motion-artifact reference), and multi-wavelength arrays for robustness.

## 3. Scientific Foundation

### 3.1 The optics
- **Beer–Lambert law:** light attenuation through an absorbing medium is $I = I_0 e^{-\varepsilon c l}$ (absorptivity × concentration × path length). Pulsatile arterial blood changes $l$ (and effective $c$) with each beat → a small time-varying absorption on top of a large constant one.
- **AC/DC decomposition:** the signal is a tiny **AC** component (pulsatile arterial blood, ~0.5–2% of total) riding on a huge **DC** component (skin, bone, venous blood, non-pulsatile tissue). Extracting a 1% wiggle from a moving wrist is the entire engineering problem in one sentence.
- **Wavelength choice:**
  - **Green (~525 nm):** strongly absorbed by hemoglobin, shallow penetration → high pulsatile contrast, **more robust to motion** → the default for wrist *heart rate*. (This is why your watch glows green.)
  - **Red (~660 nm) + Infrared (~940 nm):** deeper penetration, and the red/IR absorption *ratio* differs for oxygenated vs deoxygenated hemoglobin → required for **SpO₂** (pulse oximetry's two-wavelength ratio-of-ratios). Green cannot do SpO₂; red/IR is more motion-sensitive. The wavelength you need depends on the physiology you're after — there is no single best LED.

### 3.2 Reflectance vs transmittance
Hospital fingertip oximeters use **transmittance** (LED one side, detector the other — clean signal through a thin digit). A watch can't transmit through a wrist, so it uses **reflectance** (LED and detector side by side, reading backscattered light). Reflectance is inherently noisier, position-sensitive, and pressure-sensitive (strap tightness changes perfusion) — the price of wrist convenience, and the reason wrist SpO₂ is a screening aid, not a clinical vital.

### 3.3 The waveform, and why HR is the easy part
A clean PPG pulse has a systolic upstroke, a **dicrotic notch** (aortic valve closure), and a diastolic decay. Heart rate needs only the *peak-to-peak period* — robust, because you're finding a dominant frequency (~0.5–3 Hz). Everything richer — HRV needs precise *beat-to-beat* timing (±few ms), respiration needs the slow amplitude/baseline modulation, blood pressure needs the *pulse shape and transit time* — requires progressively cleaner signal. **The confidence ladder is an SNR ladder.**

### 3.4 The adversary: motion artifact
Wrist motion corrupts PPG three ways at once: it moves the sensor relative to skin (optical coupling changes), it changes blood perfusion (wrist flexion), and — cruelly — **cadence artifacts from walking/running can sit in the same 1–3 Hz band as heart rate**, so a naive peak-finder locks onto your *step rate* and reports it as pulse. The solution defines modern PPG: **the accelerometer is a required input to the PPG algorithm** — its motion spectrum is used as an *adaptive noise reference* to subtract cadence and coupling artifacts (adaptive filters, spectral subtraction, deep models fusing PPG+accel). Remember this: in RAXHA the accelerometer and PPG are not two independent witnesses — they are *coupled at the signal-processing layer* before either produces a feature.

## 4. Internal Working — signal path

1. **LED drive** (green for HR; red/IR added for SpO₂), current-controlled, often multiplexed across an LED array.
2. **Photodiode(s)** convert returned photons to current; **transimpedance amplifier** → voltage.
3. **Ambient-light rejection:** synchronous detection (modulate the LED, demodulate — rejects sunlight/room light, the same chopper trick you met in the accelerometer's readout); mechanical gaskets block stray light.
4. **AGC:** LED current and gain auto-adjust for skin tone, perfusion, tattoo, hair, tightness — a control loop that itself consumes power and can lag rapid changes.
5. **ADC → DSP:** filtering, accel-referenced motion cancellation, peak detection / frequency estimation → HR; richer pipelines derive HRV/SpO₂/respiration.
6. **Power reality:** LEDs dominate. Continuous high-rate multi-LED PPG is *tens of mW* — far costlier than the accelerometer's microamps and even the gyro's milliamps. **PPG is duty-cycled harder than any sensor you've met** — sampled in bursts (e.g., seconds per minute at rest), continuously only during workouts or elevated-risk states. This power fact will dominate every RAXHA design decision in §13.

## 5. Hardware

Representative: Apple Watch's optical array (green/red/IR LEDs + multiple photodiodes; Apple publicly describes the multi-LED design **[V]**); Samsung/Garmin/Fitbit analogous; sensor-vendor parts from **Maxim/Analog Devices (MAX86xxx/MAXM86xxx)** and **TI AFE44xx/AFE4900** integrate LED drivers + TIA + ADC + accel interface in one AFE. Key hardware truths: green-LED count and photodiode area drive SNR; optical isolation and skin contact are mechanical make-or-break; the AFE usually ingests accelerometer data directly for hardware-level motion reference. RAXHA inherits whatever optical stack the OEM shipped — we consume derived HR/HRV, rarely raw PPG (Apple gives third parties HR, not the raw optical waveform; **[V]**).

## 6. Software

- **iOS/watchOS — HealthKit:** `HKQuantityType` for `.heartRate`, `.heartRateVariabilitySDNN`, `.oxygenSaturation`, `.restingHeartRate`, `.walkingHeartRateAverage`; **`HKAnchoredObjectQuery`** + background delivery for updates; workouts raise sampling frequency. Raw PPG waveform is **not** exposed to third parties **[V]** — you get beats, not optics. `HKHeartbeatSeriesSample` gives beat-to-beat intervals for HRV in some contexts.
- **Android — Health Services / Health Connect:** `HeartRate` `DataType` (live via Health Services `MeasureClient` for on-demand, `PassiveMonitoringClient` for background); Health Connect as the cross-app store. Wear OS `SensorManager` exposes `TYPE_HEART_RATE`; some OEMs expose more, inconsistently (the fragmentation lesson from B3 recurs).
- **The units/latency traps:** HR arrives irregularly (event-driven, not fixed-rate — a resting watch may report every several seconds, not continuously); a "current heart rate" can be tens of seconds stale. For an emergency signal, *staleness is a safety property* — an alert reasoning on a 40-second-old HR must know it's 40 seconds old.

---

## 7. Data

- Raw PPG (when available at all): 25–125 Hz. Derived HR: irregular, seconds-apart at rest, denser in workouts.
- **Quality is not optional metadata — it is the signal.** Every PPG-derived value must carry a confidence/quality indicator (SNR, motion level, perfusion index). A heart rate without a quality flag is unusable for safety decisions.
- **Signal-quality indices (SQI):** perfusion index (AC/DC ratio), template-matching against expected pulse shape, accel-correlation (high motion → low trust). RAXHA must compute or consume SQI and gate on it — *the gated-absolute-reference pattern from the magnetometer chapter, now applied to physiology.* PPG is another witness that lies under specific, detectable conditions (motion, poor contact, low perfusion).

---

## 8. Algorithms

- **HR estimation:** time-domain peak detection (clean signal) or frequency-domain dominant-peak with accel-based cadence removal (motion). Classic method: **adaptive noise cancellation** using accel as reference; landmark academic framework **TROIKA** (Zhang et al., 2015 — spectral peak tracking with accel-aided denoising, the citation anchor for motion-tolerant wrist HR).
- **HRV:** requires beat-to-beat interval precision; time-domain (SDNN, RMSSD) and frequency-domain (LF/HF) — deep dive in Chapter 1.10, but the SNR prerequisite is set here.
- **SpO₂:** red/IR ratio-of-ratios, empirically calibrated (the calibration curve is proprietary and population-derived — a source of the skin-tone bias in §12).
- **Modern:** deep networks fusing PPG+accel for HR and artifact rejection; self-supervised pretraining on large PPG corpora (the wearable-foundation-model trend from Chapter 1.1 §10 applies here too).
- **The cascade role:** PPG does *not* trigger fall detection (too slow, too power-hungry, too noisy for onset). It **confirms and classifies** in Tier 2 — "the accelerometer says something happened; is the physiology consistent with a real medical emergency?" — and it **independently triggers** slow-onset events (sustained tachy/bradycardia, no-pulse) that motion sensors are blind to.

- **Apple [V]:** green-LED HR, red/IR SpO₂ (Series 6+, 2020), PPG-based irregular-rhythm notifications (FDA-cleared as a *screening* feature, distinct from the ECG app); high/low HR alerts. Apple exposes derived HR/HRV to apps, not raw PPG.
- **Fitbit/Google [V]:** continuous HR pioneer; PPG for sleep stages, stress (EDA combined), AFib screening (FDA-cleared algorithm).
- **Garmin [V]:** Elevate optical HR, Pulse Ox; body-battery/stress from HRV.
- **WHOOP [V]:** PPG-centric by design — no ECG, deep investment in HRV/recovery/strain from green PPG; a whole business built on *interpreting* PPG rather than adding sensors. **[I]:** their moat is the interpretation model, echoing "inference is the moat."
- **Oura [V]:** finger PPG — the finger's cleaner optical path (§3.2) is a deliberate signal-quality choice traded against wrist convenience; a beautiful illustration that *sensor placement is a product strategy.*

## 10. Research

- **Hertzman 1937** (origin); **Aoyagi 1970s** (pulse oximetry — the two-wavelength insight).
- **Allen 2007**, "Photoplethysmography and its application in clinical physiological measurement," *Physiol. Meas.* — the canonical review; read it.
- **Zhang et al. 2015, TROIKA** — motion-tolerant wrist HR benchmark; spawned a decade of accel-aided PPG work.
- **Skin-tone validity literature** (growing, urgent): documented reduced pulse-oximetry accuracy in darker skin (e.g., Sjoding et al., *NEJM* 2020, on hospital oximeters — occult hypoxemia missed disproportionately in Black patients) — a scientific-validity *and* equity finding that RAXHA cannot ethically ignore. **Open problems:** motion-robust SpO₂, cuffless BP validity, skin-tone-equitable calibration, PPG-only sleep staging honesty.

## 11. Security & Privacy

- PPG/HR is **health data** — the strictest privacy tier (HIPAA/GDPR special category). The data-plane doctrine (raw stays on-device) is now a *legal* requirement, not only an engineering one.
- **PPG is biometric and inferential:** HRV leaks stress/emotion; resting HR trends leak pregnancy, illness, drug use. Continuous HR is a behavioral fingerprint. Retention TTLs and on-device processing are the defense.
- **Attack surface:** less about spoofing (hard to fake a pulse optically at the wrist) and more about **inference privacy** — what an adversary or the *company itself* could deduce. The standing kill criterion (no monetizing sensor data) is a PPG criterion above all.

---

## 12. Medical Perspective

- **What's real:** resting HR (★★★★★), HR trends, AFib *screening* (not diagnosis) — FDA-cleared for several products. **What's aspirational:** wrist SpO₂ as a clinical vital (★★★), cuffless BP (★★).
- **Screening vs diagnosis** is the line that keeps you legal and honest: RAXHA can say "your heart rate has been unusually high" (observation); it cannot say "you have a cardiac condition" (diagnosis) without clearance.
- **Sensitivity/specificity and the base rate return:** a no-pulse or extreme-HR signal feeding an SOS decision inherits every base-rate lesson from Competency 1. A PPG that drops out because the watch loosened must **never** be read as "no pulse → cardiac arrest" — signal-loss and asystole are optically similar and catastrophically different. Distinguishing "sensor lost the signal" from "physiology stopped" is *the* PPG safety problem, and it's solved with SQI + cross-sensor context (is the person also motionless? did the accel show a fall?), never with PPG alone.
- **Skin-tone equity (§10):** a safety product whose sensitivity varies by skin color is not merely inaccurate — it is inequitable in who it protects. This is a validation requirement and an ethical one.

---

## 13. Engineering for RAXHA

1. **Power-tiered sampling:** PPG off or sparse at rest (HR every minute or on-demand); continuous only during (a) an active/suspected incident, (b) armed high-risk modes, (c) detected exertion. The accelerometer decides when PPG is worth its milliwatts — the cheap sensor gates the expensive one, a pattern now familiar.
2. **PPG as Tier-2 confirmer, not Tier-0 trigger:** after an accel fall trigger, PPG asks "is the physiology consistent with injury/distress?" — elevated or crashing HR raises confidence; calm normal HR *lowers* it (the "sat down hard, now walking, HR normal" false alarm dies here too).
3. **PPG as independent slow-event trigger:** sustained extreme brady/tachycardia or credible no-pulse (SQI-validated, motion-corroborated) *initiates* an emergency path that motion sensors would never see — this is the syncope/cardiac answer the physician demanded.
4. **Staleness and quality are first-class:** every HR value in the risk engine carries (timestamp, SQI). The engine's rules are written in terms of *validated* physiology; low-SQI values are explicitly "unknown," never silently trusted or silently ignored.
5. **The signal-loss trap is a named, tested failure case:** loss-of-signal ≠ loss-of-pulse. Require corroboration before any no-pulse escalation.

## 14. Failure Analysis

| Failure | Mechanism | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Reports step rate as heart rate | Cadence artifact in HR band | Accel-referenced cadence removal (TROIKA-class); SQI gating |
| "No pulse" from a loose watch | Signal loss read as asystole | SQI + motion/context corroboration; never PPG-alone no-pulse alerts |
| Misses low SpO₂ in darker skin | Calibration bias (§10, §12) | Skin-tone-inclusive validation; treat wrist SpO₂ as screening; disclose limits |
| Cold-hands dropout | Vasoconstriction kills peripheral perfusion | Perfusion-index gating; fall back to other evidence |
| Tattoo/hair/poor contact | Optical coupling failure | AGC; quality flags; user guidance on fit |
| Battery drain complaints | PPG left continuous | Power-tiered sampling; per-sensor power telemetry; CI duty-cycle assertions |
| Stale HR drives a decision | Event-driven, seconds-old data | Timestamp every value; staleness thresholds in the risk engine |

## 15. RAXHA Application — distilled

- **Use as:** the physiological confirmer (Tier 2) that finally addresses soft-fall/syncope, and the independent trigger for slow cardiac events motion can't see.
- **Do NOT use as:** a fall *trigger* (power, latency, noise), a diagnostic instrument (legal/scientific line), or a no-pulse alarm without corroboration (the signal-loss trap).
- **Coupled, not companion:** PPG and accelerometer fuse at the signal layer (motion reference) *and* the decision layer (context). This is RAXHA's first true multi-modal fusion, and the reason the physician's objection finally gets a real answer.

## 18. Founder Intelligence

**Why hasn't Apple solved PPG perfectly?** Because wrist reflectance PPG fights physics (1% AC signal), motion, perfusion, and skin-tone variation simultaneously — and the last one has no clean engineering fix, only better calibration and honest limits. Apple's response is fusion + conservative claims (screening, not diagnosis) + adding ECG as a *separate* higher-fidelity path. **WHOOP** built an entire company on *interpreting* green PPG better than anyone — proof that the moat is inference, not the LED (they add no ECG; they mine HRV harder). **Oura** chose the *finger* for signal quality — placement as strategy. **Garmin** ties PPG to its athletic-context business. **Google/Fitbit** turned PPG into FDA-cleared AFib screening — regulatory rigor as moat, the AliveCor lesson again. **Samsung** breadth. **Why doesn't everyone build cardiac emergency detection from PPG?** The signal-loss-vs-no-pulse trap makes false "cardiac arrest" alerts terrifying and false-reassurance deadly; the liability and validation burden is immense; skin-tone equity is a regulatory and ethical minefield. **Startup opportunities:** PPG-based cardiac screening for underserved populations, motion-robust PPG algorithms as a licensable IP layer, skin-tone-equitable calibration as a validation service, continuous physiological baselines for elder care. **RAXHA strategy:** PPG is the sensor that lets RAXHA credibly claim *medical* seriousness (not just "you fell") — but claim only what's validated (HR trends, extreme-HR alerts), keep SpO₂/BP in the research lab, and make skin-tone-equitable validation a launch gate, not a v2. **PhD gaps:** discriminating asystole from signal-loss at the wrist with quantified confidence; ## 21. Chief Scientist's Verdict

**Confidence Ledger:** Wrist PPG resting HR — ★★★★★. Motion-tolerant HR via accel fusion — ★★★★☆ (TROIKA-class methods, deployed; degrades at extremes). HRV from wrist PPG at rest — ★★★★☆. Wrist SpO₂ as screening — ★★★☆☆. PPG AFib *screening* — ★★★★☆ (FDA-cleared products). Cuffless BP from PPG — ★★☆☆☆ (research; not validated for clinical use). Discriminating no-pulse from signal-loss at the wrist — ★★☆☆☆ (**hard, under-solved — and safety-critical; the gap that most constrains RAXHA's cardiac ambitions**).
**TRL:** Wrist HR — 9. Accel-fused motion rejection — 8–9. AFib screening — 8–9 (cleared). Wrist SpO₂ clinical use — 4–5. Cuffless BP — 3–4. Wrist asystole detection — 2–3. Skin-tone-equitable SpO₂ — 4 (active problem).
**Roadmap:** *MVP:* HR trends + extreme-HR alerts as Tier-2 confirmation and slow-event triggers (SQI-gated, corroborated). *V2:* HRV baselines, AFib-screening integration where platform-provided. *Research Lab:* wrist SpO₂ for emergencies, cuffless BP, standalone asystole detection. *Never Build (yet):* automated cardiac-arrest alerts from PPG alone (the signal-loss trap makes this ★★ and lethal-if-wrong); any diagnostic claim without clearance; skin-tone-blind SpO₂ shipping.
**Competitor failures (sourced):** documented pulse-oximeter skin-tone bias (Sjoding *NEJM* 2020) — a whole sensor class shipped with an equity flaw hidden for decades; wrist-HR accuracy complaints during high-intensity/cold conditions (widely documented in reviews) — motion and perfusion remain unsolved at the edges; consumer SpO₂ features quietly caveated or (for Apple, due to a *patent* dispute) disabled in some regions — a reminder that even validated features carry IP/regulatory fragility.
**Kill Criteria:** If SQI-gated no-pulse detection can't achieve a specificity that keeps false cardiac alerts below a tiny fixed rate in shadow mode → do not ship automated cardiac alerts; keep PPG as confirmation-only. If skin-tone-stratified validation shows materially worse sensitivity for any group → the feature does not launch until closed. If PPG power exceeds the battery budget users tolerate → tighten duty-cycling before adding any continuous-PPG feature.
**Historical Failures (Historian):** Pulse-oximetry skin-tone bias (above) — the canonical "validated for decades, inequitable all along" failure; the antidote is stratified validation, now a RAXHA launch gate. Consumer BP-from-PPG overclaims (multiple startups marketed cuffless BP ahead of validation; regulatory pushback) — the ★★-claim-with-★★★★★-marketing pattern the Confidence Ledger exists to stop. Early wrist-HR trackers panned in reviews for exercise inaccuracy — shipping the easy rung (resting HR) while implying the hard rungs, a credibility wound RAXHA avoids by stating which rung each claim sits on.
