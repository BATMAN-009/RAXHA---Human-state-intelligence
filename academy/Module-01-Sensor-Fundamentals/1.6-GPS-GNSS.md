# Chapter 1.6 — GPS / GNSS

> **Why this chapter turns detection into rescue.** Every prior sensor answered *what is happening to the body*. This one answers *where the body is* — and a detected emergency without a location is only half a rescue. RAXHA's manifesto promise ("alerts trusted family with the user's live location") lives or dies here. But GPS is also the first sensor whose fundamental error mode is *confident wrongness*: it will hand you a precise-looking coordinate that is 200 meters off, indoors, in a city, exactly where emergencies happen. The gated-absolute-reference discipline you built for the magnetometer now faces its hardest test.

---

## 1. Definition

- **GPS** (Global Positioning System) is the United States' satellite navigation constellation. **GNSS** (Global Navigation Satellite System) is the generic term for *all* of them: GPS (USA), **GLONASS** (Russia), **Galileo** (EU), **BeiDou** (China), plus regional systems (QZSS Japan, NavIC India). A modern phone/watch chip is **multi-constellation** — it listens to all simultaneously, which is why urban fixes have improved dramatically.
- The receiver computes its position by measuring the **time-of-flight** of signals from multiple satellites whose positions and clocks are known, then solving for its own 3D position **and its own clock error** — four unknowns (x, y, z, t), so **four satellites minimum**; more satellites = better geometry = better accuracy.
- **A-GPS** (Assisted GPS) uses the cellular network to deliver satellite orbit/almanac data and a rough initial position, collapsing the "time to first fix" from *minutes* (cold start) to *seconds*. This is a latency fact RAXHA cannot ignore — an emergency that has to wait 45 seconds for a cold GPS fix is an emergency that arrives late.

## 2. History

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1973→ | US DoD begins GPS (building on Transit, 1960s) | Military positioning; the enabling idea: atomic clocks in orbit |
| 1983 | Reagan opens GPS to civilian use (after KAL 007) | Policy decision that created the entire consumer-location industry |
| 1978–94 | Full GPS constellation deployed | 24+ satellites, global coverage |
| 2000 | **Selective Availability turned off** | Civilian accuracy jumps from ~100 m to ~10 m overnight — the moment consumer GPS became viable |
| 2005→ | A-GPS in phones; GLONASS revived; Galileo & BeiDou built | Multi-constellation era |
| 2018→ | **Dual-frequency (L1+L5) GNSS** in consumer phones (Broadcom BCM47755, Xiaomi Mi 8 first) | The biggest accuracy leap since SA-off: L5 resists multipath, the urban-canyon killer |
| Now | Every phone/LTE-watch is multi-constellation, A-GPS, often dual-frequency | Meter-class outdoors; still meters-to-hundreds-of-meters in cities and useless indoors |

**Current state:** superb open-sky accuracy (1–5 m), degraded urban (tens of meters), effectively unavailable indoors. **Trend:** dual-frequency mainstream, GNSS+inertial fusion, PPP/RTK creeping toward consumer for centimeter accuracy.

## 3. Scientific Foundation

### 3.1 Trilateration by time-of-flight
Each satellite broadcasts its position and a precise time. The receiver measures signal delay → distance ("pseudorange") to each satellite. With enough pseudoranges, it solves the intersection of spheres. The catch: the receiver's clock is a cheap quartz oscillator, wrong by microseconds — and light travels 300 m per microsecond, so clock error is *the* dominant error unless solved for. Hence the fourth satellite: it turns the clock error into a solvable unknown. **Takeaway: GPS is fundamentally a clock-synchronization problem wearing a positioning costume** — relativity matters (satellite clocks are corrected for both special and general relativistic effects, ~38 µs/day, or the system would drift kilometers daily).

### 3.2 The error budget (know these — they define RAXHA's location trust)
- **Ionospheric/tropospheric delay:** signal slows through the atmosphere; the largest natural error; dual-frequency (L1/L5) largely cancels it.
- **Multipath:** signals bounce off buildings and arrive late, inflating pseudorange — **the urban-canyon curse.** L5's signal structure resists it; still the dominant city error.
- **Satellite geometry (DOP — Dilution of Precision):** satellites clustered in one patch of sky → poor triangulation → large error even with good signals. Reported as HDOP/PDOP.
- **Ephemeris/clock errors:** small residual satellite-side errors.
- **The number that matters:** the reported "accuracy" (e.g., `horizontalAccuracy`) is an *estimated 68% confidence radius*, not a guarantee — and it is frequently *optimistic* in multipath. **RAXHA must treat accuracy as a probabilistic hint, never a promise.**

### 3.3 Why indoors is hopeless (for GPS)
Satellite signals are ~ -160 dBW at the ground — below the noise floor, recovered only by correlation. Walls attenuate them below recoverability. Indoors, GPS either fails or (worse) locks onto multipath ghosts and reports a confident, wrong position. Indoor positioning is a *different technology stack* (Wi-Fi/BLE/UWB — Chapter 1.7), which is why that chapter exists.

## 4. Internal Working — signal path

1. **RF front-end + correlators:** despread the satellite codes (CDMA), acquire and track each satellite's signal.
2. **Pseudorange + carrier-phase measurement** per satellite.
3. **A-GPS assist:** network supplies ephemeris + rough position + time → fast acquisition.
4. **Navigation solution:** least-squares / Kalman filter solving position+clock, weighted by signal quality and geometry.
5. **GNSS+inertial fusion (dead reckoning):** the accelerometer/gyro (Chapters 1.1–1.2) bridge GPS gaps — tunnels, urban canyons — by propagating the last known position with motion. This is why your map dot keeps moving in a tunnel. (Fusion deep-dive: Module 8.)
6. **Output:** position, accuracy estimate, speed (from Doppler — often *more* reliable than position-derived speed), heading, timestamp.

**Power reality:** the GNSS receiver is **one of the most power-hungry sensors on the device** — tens of mW, continuous active-tracking can dominate battery. This dwarfs the accelerometer and rivals/exceeds continuous PPG. **GPS is duty-cycled and event-driven, never casually left on** — the master lesson of §13.

---

## 5. Hardware

Integrated into the cellular/connectivity SoC (Qualcomm, Broadcom) rather than a discrete part; multi-constellation, increasingly dual-frequency. Antenna quality and placement dominate real-world performance (a watch's tiny antenna underperforms a phone's). LTE watches have independent GNSS; non-LTE watches often rely on the phone's location via BLE (a coupling that matters for the "watch alone" autonomy doctrine — a phone-tethered watch has no independent location). RAXHA inherits the chip; what RAXHA controls is *when* to request location, at *what* accuracy/power tier, and *how much to trust* the result.

---

## 6. Software

- **iOS — Core Location:** `CLLocationManager`; **accuracy tiers** (`kCLLocationAccuracyBest`, `...HundredMeters`, `...Reduced`) trade power for precision; **`CLLocation`** carries `horizontalAccuracy`, `speed`, `course`, `timestamp`. **`startMonitoringSignificantLocationChanges`** (cell-tower-based, very low power, relaunches your app even after termination) and **region monitoring / `CLCircularRegion`** (geofencing at the OS level) are the background-friendly primitives. iOS 14+ adds **precise vs approximate** location as a user choice. `CLLocationManager.allowsBackgroundLocationUpdates` + the `location` background mode is the sanctioned continuous path.
- **Android — Fused Location Provider (FusedLocationProviderClient):** Google Play Services' fusion of GPS+Wi-Fi+cell+sensors; `Priority.HIGH_ACCURACY` vs `BALANCED_POWER` vs `PASSIVE`; **Geofencing API**; background location requires the separate `ACCESS_BACKGROUND_LOCATION` permission (Android 10+), granted in Settings, with Play policy scrutiny. Raw GNSS measurements available via `GnssMeasurement` for advanced use.
- **The permission reality (both platforms):** background location is the most scrutinized, most-denied permission there is, because it's the most abused. RAXHA's *entire location value* depends on a permission users are trained to refuse — a product and trust problem (§13, B6), not a coding one.

---

## 7. Data

- A `Location` = {lat, lon, horizontalAccuracy, altitude+verticalAccuracy, speed, course, timestamp, provider}.
- **Accuracy is a field, not a fact:** every location carries its own uncertainty; RAXHA must propagate it. "User is here" is really "user is within ~R meters of here, 68% of the time." A 15 m accuracy and a 200 m accuracy are *different kinds of evidence*.
- **Staleness (Decision #13 again):** a cached location can be minutes old; Core Location/Fused will hand you the last known fix instantly, which may predate the emergency. Every location used in an alert must carry and check its age.
- **Speed from Doppler** is often more trustworthy than position — useful for crash detection (sudden deceleration) and driving-context gating.

---

## 8. Algorithms

- **Accuracy-weighted filtering:** Kalman-filter positions weighted by reported accuracy; reject/downweight implausible jumps (a 500 m teleport between 1 s samples is multipath, not motion).
- **Map-matching:** snap noisy positions to road/path networks (raises apparent accuracy in context).
- **Dead reckoning:** inertial propagation through GPS outages (Module 8).
- **Geofencing logic:** enter/exit/dwell with hysteresis (a geofence without hysteresis flaps at the boundary — the same lesson as the magnetometer gate). Debounce + minimum-dwell to avoid false crossings from GPS jitter.
- **RAXHA's location-fusion role:** location is Tier-2 *context* — it rarely triggers, it *enriches and routes*. "Fall detected + user is on a highway at 70 mph" → likely vehicle context; "no movement for an hour + at home" vs "at an unfamiliar location" changes escalation. And critically, location is the *payload* of the alert, not usually the trigger.

## 9. Real Products

- **Apple [V]:** Emergency SOS with location; crash detection fuses GPS speed with impact/pressure/audio; Find My; satellite SOS (Emergency SOS via satellite, 2022) for when there's no cell — a direct model for RAXHA's "no network" degraded path.
- **Google [V]:** Fused Location, Personal Safety app with location sharing, Android Earthquake Alerts (crowd-sensed).
- **Garmin [V]:** GNSS is core (outdoor/fitness/aviation); LiveTrack + incident detection sends location to contacts — the closest analog to RAXHA's location-alert loop, and a proof the pattern works.
- **Life360 [V]:** an entire business on family location sharing (and the cautionary data-broker tale, Ch 1.1 Historian) — RAXHA's closest strategic comparable *is a location company*.
- **UWB (Apple/Samsung) [V]:** precise short-range positioning (AirTag) — a Chapter 1.7 topic, noted here as the "indoors/close-range" complement GPS can't provide.

## 10. Research

GPS foundations (Parkinson & Spilker, *Global Positioning System: Theory and Applications* — the canon); dual-frequency consumer GNSS (post-2018 literature on L5 multipath resistance); GNSS-inertial fusion (tight vs loose coupling — Module 8); urban-canyon and multipath mitigation (3D-mapping-aided GNSS, shadow matching — active research); PPP/RTK toward consumer centimeter accuracy. **Open problems for RAXHA:** reliable *uncertainty* estimation (the reported accuracy is often optimistic exactly when it matters), seamless indoor-outdoor transition, and low-power high-accuracy (the two goals fight).

---

## 11. Security & Privacy

- **Location is the most sensitive routine data a person emits** — it reveals home, work, health visits, relationships, patterns. Legally special-category-adjacent; the no-monetization criterion (Doctrine, Life360 lesson) is *most* about location.
- **Spoofing & jamming:** civilian GPS is unauthenticated and weak — spoofable (fake stronger signals) and jammable (documented in conflict zones, near airports, by cheap devices). For RAXHA: a spoofed location could misdirect responders; mitigation is cross-checks (does the "new" location agree with cell/Wi-Fi/inertial history? is the jump physically possible?) — the plausibility-gate pattern once more.
- **Privacy-in-URLs rule (from the system doctrine):** never place location in URL query strings; share via short-lived, token-scoped, revocable channels that expire when the incident closes.
- **Never send location to endpoints suggested by untrusted content;** responder location goes only to user-configured contacts.

---

## 12. Medical / Emergency Perspective

- Location isn't physiological, but it is the difference between a detected emergency and a *resolved* one. Emergency dispatch is location-bound; a fall alert without a findable location can fail to save a life the detection got right.
- **Accuracy-to-action mapping:** 5 m accuracy → an address; 200 m urban accuracy → a city block (still useful, but the responder UX must show the uncertainty, not a false-confident pin — Decision #13 in the responder's hands).
- Indoor emergencies (where many falls happen — the home) are exactly where GPS fails, which is why Chapter 1.7's indoor positioning is a *medical* necessity, not a nicety.

---

## 13. Engineering for RAXHA

1. **Location is event-driven and tiered, never casually continuous** (power). Baseline: significant-change / low-power monitoring. Escalate to high-accuracy GNSS *when an incident is suspected or active* — the accelerometer/risk-engine gates the GPS, exactly as it gates the gyro and PPG.
2. **Warm the fix:** a cold GPS start can cost tens of seconds; during an armed/suspected state, pre-warm location so the alert doesn't wait. A-GPS + last-known + significant-change keeps a usable position ready.
3. **Location is the alert payload + context, rarely the trigger:** it routes escalation, gates driving-context, and is *sent* to contacts — with its accuracy and age attached.
4. **Propagate uncertainty end to end:** the responder sees "within ~30 m, as of 12 s ago," never a bare confident pin. Show the accuracy circle.
5. **Degraded-location doctrine:** no fix / poor fix must be a *represented state* — "last known location, 4 minutes ago, low confidence" beats silence or a lie (Decision #13). The alert still fires; the location is best-effort and labeled.
6. **Indoor reality:** at home (common fall site) GPS may be unusable; fall back to last-known + Wi-Fi/BLE context (Ch 1.7) + the user's registered home address.
7. **Watch autonomy:** an LTE watch must fetch its own location for a phone-absent SOS; a tethered watch's location plan must be explicit.

## 14. Failure Analysis

| Failure | Mechanism | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Confident wrong position in a city | Multipath / poor DOP, optimistic accuracy | Dual-frequency; accuracy-weighted rejection; show uncertainty; cross-check cell/Wi-Fi |
| Alert sent with stale location | Cached last-known returned instantly | Timestamp + staleness threshold; pre-warm during armed state |
| No location at all (indoors) | Signal below noise floor | Last-known + Wi-Fi/BLE + registered address; label as degraded |
| 45 s wait for first fix | Cold start, no assist | A-GPS; keep a warm fix in armed/suspected states |
| Battery drain | GPS left in high-accuracy continuous | Tiered/event-driven; power telemetry; CI duty-cycle assertions |
| Spoofed/jammed location | Unauthenticated civilian GNSS | Plausibility cross-checks (inertial/cell/Wi-Fi consistency) |
| Geofence flapping | GPS jitter at boundary | Hysteresis + dwell time + accuracy gating |
| Watch has no location | Phone-tethered, phone absent | LTE-watch independent GNSS; explicit tethered-fallback UX |

## 15. RAXHA Application — distilled

- **Use as:** the alert *payload* (where to send help) and Tier-2 *context* (driving? home? unfamiliar place?). The thing that makes a detected emergency actionable.
- **Do NOT use as:** a primary trigger (except crash-relevant speed/deceleration), a trusted indoor position, or a confident pin without its uncertainty. Never leave it continuously on.
- **The one-liner:** *GPS doesn't detect the emergency — it makes the emergency findable, and only as findable as its honestly-reported uncertainty allows.*

## 16. Future

Dual-frequency everywhere; consumer PPP/RTK toward centimeter accuracy; tight GNSS-inertial-visual fusion for seamless indoor-outdoor; better on-device uncertainty estimation; satellite-direct messaging (Emergency SOS via satellite) generalizing — a profound shift for a safety product that must work off-grid.

## 18. Founder Intelligence

**Why hasn't Apple "solved" location perfectly?** Because multipath and indoor attenuation are physics, and civilian GNSS is unauthenticated by design — no chip fixes urban-canyon confident-wrongness or indoor unavailability alone. Apple's answers are fusion (Wi-Fi/BLE/UWB/inertial), honest accuracy reporting, and *satellite SOS* for the off-grid case. **Garmin** built a business on GNSS excellence (dedicated antennas, battery discipline) for outdoor/aviation — a vertical where location *is* the product. **Life360** is the strategic mirror: a multi-billion-dollar company on *family location*, whose worst wound was monetizing that location (the Historian's cautionary tale). **Google** owns the fused-location + maps + places graph; location is platform leverage. **Why doesn't everyone build location-based safety?** Background-location permission is the most-denied on both platforms; the privacy liability is severe; indoor accuracy (where home emergencies happen) is unsolved by GPS; and battery cost is punishing. **Startup opportunities:** privacy-preserving family safety (the anti-Life360 — location that's never sold, provably), indoor-emergency location for care facilities, off-grid safety (satellite/LoRa), responder-facing precise-location tools. **RAXHA strategy:** location is the *payload* that makes detection actionable and a core of the family-safety value prop — but it's also the single most sensitive asset RAXHA holds, so "we never sell or mine your location" is not a policy footnote, it's a *primary marketing pillar and trust moat* (learn directly from Life360's wound). Keep GPS event-driven; invest in honest uncertainty and the indoor-fallback stack. **PhD gaps:** trustworthy on-device location uncertainty; seamless indoor-outdoor; spoofing-resistant consumer positioning; privacy-preserving location sharing (share presence/emergency without exposing continuous history). **Patents:** A-GPS and fused-location families (Qualcomm, Broadcom, Google — query `assisted GPS fused location assignee:X`); geofencing families; UWB ranging (Apple/NXP). Much GNSS *method* is government/public; consumer IP is in fusion, power management, and assist. **Ledger:** ✅ constellations, A-GPS, dual-frequency, APIs, satellite SOS; 🟡 exact fusion/assist algorithms; 🔴 vendor accuracy-estimation internals.

## 19. Design Review (highlights)

- **Emergency physician:** "A fall at home is your commonest serious case — and it's exactly where GPS dies. If your whole location story is GPS, you've optimized for the rare outdoor case and neglected the common indoor one. Show me the indoor plan." *(→ Ch 1.7 + registered address; this is why the next competency exists.)*
- **Privacy advocate:** "Location is the asset you can most easily betray users with. Show me it's never sold, retention is minimal, sharing is short-lived and revocable, and it never appears in a URL."
- **Garmin engineer:** "GPS is your biggest battery line item. Continuous high-accuracy will halve your watch life. Where's the tiered power table with measured mW?"
- **Security researcher:** "Civilian GPS is spoofable. If I feed your watch a fake location, do you misdirect the ambulance? Prove your plausibility cross-checks."
- **Investor:** "Life360 proved families pay for location. Your differentiator is 'we don't sell it.' Is privacy-as-a-feature a real moat or a slide?" *(Answer: post-scandal, provable non-monetization is increasingly a real wedge — but it must be architectural, not promissory.)*

## 21. Chief Scientist's Verdict

**Confidence Ledger:** Open-sky GNSS accuracy 1–5 m — ★★★★★. Dual-frequency multipath reduction — ★★★★☆. Reported `horizontalAccuracy` as a *reliable* bound — ★★☆☆☆ (**often optimistic in multipath — the key trust gap**). GPS usable indoors — ★☆☆☆☆ (essentially not). Speed-from-Doppler reliability — ★★★★☆. A-GPS fast-fix — ★★★★★. GNSS-inertial dead reckoning through short outages — ★★★★☆.
**TRL:** Consumer multi-constellation GNSS — 9. A-GPS — 9. Dual-frequency consumer — 8–9. Geofencing — 9. Consumer indoor positioning via GPS — 1–2 (wrong tool). Trustworthy on-device uncertainty — 5–6.
**Roadmap:** *MVP:* event-driven tiered location as alert payload + driving/home context; last-known + registered-address indoor fallback; expiring-token sharing. *V2:* dual-frequency exploitation, GNSS-inertial fusion for continuity, satellite/off-grid path. *Research:* honest uncertainty estimation, seamless indoor-outdoor. *Never Build:* continuous high-accuracy GPS (battery); confident pins without uncertainty; any server-side location retention beyond the incident; monetized location, ever.
**Competitor failures (sourced):** Life360's documented location-data-broker sale (2021 press) — the definitive "monetized the trust asset" failure; RAXHA's architecture must make this *impossible*, not merely disallowed. Consumer navigation's well-documented urban-canyon errors (users routed onto parallel streets) — confident-wrong location is a visible, trust-eroding failure. E911 location-accuracy shortfalls (regulatory scrutiny of carrier indoor-location accuracy) — even mandated emergency location struggles indoors; a sober benchmark for RAXHA's ambitions.
**Kill Criteria:** if indoor-emergency location can't reach actionable confidence for the home case, scope claims to "shares best-known location" and lean on registered addresses — don't imply precision you can't deliver where falls actually happen. If the location power budget can't be met without degrading fix-readiness during incidents, narrow armed-mode scope rather than ship a late-location alert. If non-monetization can't be made architecturally provable, the privacy-moat claim doesn't ship as marketing.
**Historical Failures (Historian):** Life360 data-broker sale (above). Google/Apple's early location-privacy scandals (background location collection controversies, documented) — the reputational template; "always-knows-where-you-are" is a trust liability unless consent and control are impeccable. Sidewalk/■ smart-city location projects abandoned partly over location-privacy backlash — location features die from trust failures more often than technical ones.
