# Chapter 1.10 — Heart Rate & Heart-Rate Variability (HRV)

> **Why this chapter is pivotal.** PPG (Chapter 1.8) gave you the raw pulse. This chapter turns it into *physiological intelligence* — and introduces the single most important idea in all of RAXHA's detection strategy: **the baseline.** Heart rate tells you the body's state now; HRV tells you the autonomic nervous system's balance; and a *personalized* baseline tells you whether "now" is normal *for this person*. Every anomaly-detection module later in the curriculum stands on the concept you learn here: population thresholds are weak, personal deviations are strong.

---

## 1. Definition

- **Heart rate (HR):** beats per minute — the rate of cardiac contractions. Simple, robust, ★★★★★ from wrist PPG at rest.
- **Heart-rate variability (HRV):** the variation in time *between consecutive heartbeats* (the R–R interval in ECG, or the pulse-to-pulse "P–P"/inter-beat interval in PPG). Counterintuitively, a *healthy* heart is **not** metronomic — the intervals fluctuate beat to beat, and *more* variability is generally *better* (a sign of a responsive autonomic nervous system). HRV is a window onto the **autonomic nervous system (ANS)**: the balance between sympathetic ("fight or flight," speeds and regularizes the heart) and parasympathetic ("rest and digest," via the vagus nerve, slows and varies it).

The conceptual leap: HR is a *number*; HRV is a *statistic of a time series*; the baseline is a *personal distribution*. Each level up carries more information and more fragility.

---

## 2. History

Fetal HRV monitoring (1960s obstetrics — reduced variability signals fetal distress) was the first clinical use. **Task Force of the European Society of Cardiology & NASPE, 1996** — "Heart rate variability: standards of measurement, physiological interpretation, and clinical use" (*Circulation*) — is the canonical standardization paper that defined the metrics everyone still uses (SDNN, RMSSD, LF, HF). HRV as a *cardiac risk* marker (reduced HRV predicts mortality post-myocardial-infarction) is well established clinically. The wearable era (WHOOP ~2015 onward, then everyone) took HRV from a clinical/research measure to a consumer "recovery/stress" metric — with a large and honest gap between the ECG-derived clinical evidence and the wrist-PPG-derived consumer claims (§12).

---

## 3. Scientific Foundation

### 3.1 What modulates the heart
- **Sympathetic ↑** → faster, more regular (HRV down): exercise, stress, fear, pain, standing, fever, hypovolemia.
- **Parasympathetic (vagal) ↑** → slower, more variable (HRV up): rest, sleep, recovery, deep breathing.
- **Respiratory sinus arrhythmia (RSA):** HR naturally rises on inhalation and falls on exhalation — a vagally-mediated rhythm that is a *major* component of short-term HRV and the physiological bridge to respiration estimation (Chapter 1.12). This is why HRV and breathing are entangled.

### 3.2 The metrics (know these cold)
**Time-domain:**
- **SDNN** — standard deviation of NN (normal-to-normal) intervals; overall variability. Needs a stable window (often 5 min or 24 h clinically).
- **RMSSD** — root mean square of successive differences; captures *short-term, beat-to-beat* (largely parasympathetic) variability. **The wearable favorite** because it's computable on short windows and is relatively robust — WHOOP/Oura/Apple lean on RMSSD-like measures.
- **pNN50** — proportion of successive intervals differing by >50 ms.

**Frequency-domain** (power spectral density of the interval series):
- **HF (0.15–0.4 Hz)** — parasympathetic/respiratory.
- **LF (0.04–0.15 Hz)** — mixed sympathetic + parasympathetic (interpreting LF as "pure sympathetic" is a common ★★ oversimplification — flag it).
- **LF/HF ratio** — loosely "sympathovagal balance"; use with caution, it's contested.

**Nonlinear:** Poincaré plot (SD1/SD2), sample/approximate entropy, DFA — richer but data-hungry.

### 3.3 Why context is everything
An HRV number is meaningless without: **measurement conditions** (HRV is highest during sleep, suppressed when active — the near-universal consumer convention is *nightly/resting* HRV precisely to control this), **posture** (orthostatic changes shift it), **respiration rate** (slow breathing inflates HF), **age/sex/fitness**, and **the individual's own baseline**. **Absolute HRV values are nearly useless across people** (healthy adults range from ~20 ms to >100 ms RMSSD); *deviation from personal baseline* is the signal. This is the chapter's thesis and RAXHA's detection philosophy in one sentence.

---

## 4. Internal Working — from pulse to HRV

1. **Beat detection:** find each pulse peak (or ECG R-peak) precisely — HRV needs *millisecond* timing, so peak-localization error is the dominant error source at the wrist.
2. **Inter-beat interval (IBI) series:** the sequence of gaps.
3. **Artifact correction:** missed/extra/ectopic beats create spurious intervals that *destroy* HRV metrics (one missed beat doubles an interval → huge false variability). Correction = detect + interpolate/remove ectopics and artifacts. **This step matters more than the metric choice** — bad IBI cleaning invalidates everything downstream.
4. **Windowing + metric computation** (time/frequency/nonlinear).
5. **Normalization against baseline** (the RAXHA-relevant step; §8).

**PPG vs ECG for HRV:** ECG gives clean R-peaks → gold-standard "HRV." PPG gives "**PRV**" (pulse-rate variability), which *approximates* HRV but is blurred by pulse-transit-time variation and is far more motion-sensitive. At rest they agree well; during motion/stress PRV diverges from true HRV. Honest products compute HRV only in still conditions (sleep) — a signal-quality gate again.

---

## 5–6. Hardware & Software (brief — mostly inherited from 1.8)

Hardware: same PPG optics (green for HR; clean IBI needs good SNR) or ECG electrodes (Chapter 1.9) for gold-standard. Software: **iOS** exposes `HKQuantityType.heartRateVariabilitySDNN` and `HKHeartbeatSeriesSample` (beat-to-beat intervals) — Apple computes SDNN over short windows and stores it; you consume it, you don't get raw IBIs continuously. **Android/Wear OS**: Health Services HR + some HRV/IBI depending on device (fragmented — capability-detect); Health Connect has HR records, HRV support varies. **The trap (Doctrine #12, platform truth):** each platform defines *which* HRV metric, over *what* window, under *what* conditions — and it changes across OS versions. Never assume "HRV" means the same thing across platforms or releases; re-verify, and record the definition alongside the value.

---

## 7. Data

- HR: irregular, seconds-apart at rest (event-driven, from 1.8).
- HRV: typically one value per measurement session (e.g., nightly), *not* a continuous stream — because it needs a stable multi-minute still window.
- **Every HRV value must carry:** its metric type (SDNN vs RMSSD — not interchangeable), window length, measurement condition (sleep/rest/active), and quality. An HRV value without this metadata is uninterpretable and unsafe to compare.

---

## 8. Algorithms — the baseline is the product

- **Personal baseline modeling:** maintain a rolling per-user distribution of resting HR and nightly HRV (mean + spread, often with trend). Deviation is scored in **standard deviations from personal baseline**, not absolute thresholds. A resting HR of 95 is normal for one person, a 25-point elevation for another — only the personal model distinguishes them.
- **Trend vs acute:** slow baseline drift (fitness change, chronic illness, pregnancy) vs acute deviation (acute illness onset, stress, cardiac event). Different time constants, different meanings.
- **Illness/anomaly pre-detection:** elevated resting HR + suppressed HRV over successive nights is a documented early signal of infection onset (multiple studies used wearables to flag COVID/flu before symptoms) — ★★★ evidence, promising, not a diagnosis.
- **Feeding the risk engine:** in an incident, "HR 40 SD above this person's baseline" is far stronger evidence than "HR = 140." Personalization is what makes physiology usable for emergency confidence.
- **Preview of Module 9:** this personal-baseline-deviation approach *is* anomaly detection — you're building the intuition now that formalizes later (Gaussian baselines → change-point detection → autoencoders).

## 9. Real Products

- **WHOOP [V]:** nightly RMSSD-based HRV as the core of "recovery" — an entire business on interpreting one metric well against personal baseline. **[I]** the baseline modeling *is* the moat.
- **Oura [V]:** nightly HRV/HR from finger PPG (cleaner signal, §1.8) for readiness.
- **Apple [V]:** SDNN in Health; resting/walking HR; high/low HR and irregular-rhythm notifications.
- **Garmin [V]:** HRV status, stress (from HRV), body battery.
- Common thread: all report *your trend vs your normal*, not absolute numbers to compare with friends — a product convention that encodes the science of §3.3.

---

## 10. Research

Task Force 1996 (the standard); post-MI reduced-HRV mortality literature (Kleiger et al. 1987 — reduced HRV predicts mortality); wearable illness-prediction studies (e.g., Mishra et al. 2020, *Nature Biomedical Engineering*, pre-symptomatic COVID detection from wearable HR/HRV); ongoing PRV-vs-HRV validity work. **Open problems:** motion-robust HRV, PRV↔HRV reconciliation, HRV's real predictive value for *acute* events (strong for chronic risk, weaker for moment-to-moment emergencies — an honest limitation for RAXHA), and personalized-baseline methods that adapt without drifting.

---

## 11. Security & Privacy

HRV is among the most *inferential* signals on the body: it leaks stress, emotion, sleep quality, illness, pregnancy, intoxication, and mental-health state. Continuous HRV is a psychophysiological fingerprint. This raises the privacy stakes above even HR — retention minimization, on-device processing, and the no-monetization criterion apply with full force. An adversary (or an unscrupulous operator) with HRV trends could infer far more than heart health.

---

## 12. Medical Perspective

- **Strong (★★★★):** reduced HRV as a *chronic* cardiac/mortality risk marker (ECG-derived, clinical). Resting HR trends as a health indicator.
- **Promising (★★★):** wearable HR/HRV for illness pre-detection; stress estimation.
- **Weak/contested (★★):** LF/HF as clean sympathovagal balance; single-session consumer HRV as actionable; PRV = HRV under motion.
- **The RAXHA-critical honesty:** HRV is a better *chronic risk/context* signal than an *acute emergency trigger*. It enriches confidence and enables illness pre-warning; it is not, by itself, a reliable "emergency now" detector. Design accordingly — HRV shapes baselines and context, HR extremes and no-pulse (with corroboration) drive acute triggers.
- Screening-vs-diagnosis line (from 1.8) holds absolutely.

---

## 13. Engineering for RAXHA

1. **Build the personal baseline early and treat it as core infrastructure** — resting HR and nightly HRV distributions per user, updated with sensible time constants, stored as durable health-record data (B4's system-of-record). This baseline feeds *every* future anomaly module.
2. **HRV is a context/baseline input, not an acute trigger.** In the risk engine, HRV informs "what's normal for this person" and contributes to slow illness-trend warnings; it does not fire SOS on its own.
3. **HR extremes are triggers (corroborated):** sustained tachy/bradycardia *relative to personal baseline and context* (not exercising, corroborated by immobility) can initiate a check → escalation — the slow-cardiac path from 1.8, now personalized.
4. **Metadata discipline:** every HR/HRV value carries metric type, window, condition, SQI, timestamp (from 1.8/1.10). The engine reasons on *validated, contextualized* values only.
5. **Cold-start honesty:** a new user has no baseline. Use conservative population priors, widen uncertainty, and *say* protection is calibrating — never pretend precision you haven't earned (Decision #13: don't confuse "no baseline yet" with "normal").

## 14. Failure Analysis

| Failure | Mechanism | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Spurious HRV spike | One missed/ectopic beat doubled an interval | Rigorous IBI artifact correction *before* metrics |
| Cross-person comparison nonsense | Compared absolute HRV between users | Personal-baseline deviations only |
| "Recovery bad" every night | PRV corrupted by motion/poor sleep contact | Compute HRV only in validated still windows |
| False illness alarm | Baseline drift (travel, alcohol, poor sleep) read as pathology | Multi-night trends, wide priors, non-alarming framing |
| Metric confusion | SDNN vs RMSSD compared as if equal | Carry metric type; never mix |
| Cold-start overconfidence | Judged a new user against population as if personal | Conservative priors + "calibrating" state |
| HRV drove an acute SOS | Over-trusted HRV as emergency trigger | HRV = context/baseline; extremes+corroboration = trigger |

---

## 15. RAXHA Application — distilled

- **Use HR as:** a corroborating acute signal (extremes vs personal baseline, context-gated) and a Tier-2 confirmer.
- **Use HRV as:** the engine of *personalization and chronic-trend/illness pre-warning* — the thing that makes every other detector smarter by defining "normal for this person." Not an acute trigger.
- **The baseline is the crown jewel:** RAXHA's long-term intelligence compounds from per-user physiological baselines. This is also a moat (it improves with tenure) and a privacy responsibility (it's deeply revealing).

## 18. Founder Intelligence

**Why hasn't Apple "solved" HRV?** Because HRV's *acute* predictive value is genuinely limited by science, not engineering — it's a strong chronic-risk marker and a weak moment-to-moment emergency signal, and no sensor upgrade changes that. Apple ships it as a number in Health, conservatively. **WHOOP** is the definitive case study: a billion-dollar company built almost entirely on *interpreting nightly HRV against personal baseline* — no ECG, no dozen-sensor arms race, just relentless focus on one metric's baseline modeling and the coaching narrative around it. **This is "inference is the moat" in its purest commercial form.** **Oura** chose finger placement for cleaner IBIs — signal quality as strategy. **Garmin** wraps HRV in athletic recovery. **Why doesn't everyone build illness pre-detection?** The evidence is ★★★ (promising, not diagnostic), the false-alarm/anxiety cost is high, and the regulatory line between "your resting HR is elevated" (observation, fine) and "you may have an infection" (diagnosis, cleared) is sharp. **Startup opportunities:** personalized illness-pre-warning for immunocompromised/elderly, HRV-based stress/mental-health tools, athletic recovery, perioperative/post-discharge monitoring, HRV baselining as licensable infrastructure. **RAXHA strategy:** the personal baseline is simultaneously RAXHA's *intelligence engine* (makes acute detection smarter), a *compounding moat* (better the longer a user stays — the anti-churn asset), and a *wellness-subscription surface* (daily engagement distinct from the safety alarm). But keep HRV in its evidentiary lane: context and trend, not acute trigger. **PhD gaps:** proving/bounding HRV's acute-event predictive value; motion-robust HRV; adaptive personal baselines that don't drift into pathology-blindness; federated baseline learning. **Patents:** WHOOP/Oura recovery-and-baseline families (query `heart rate variability recovery baseline assignee:X`); illness-detection-from-wearable families (query `wearable infection detection heart rate variability`); note much HRV *method* is old/public (Task Force 1996) — the IP is in application and baseline modeling, not the metrics. **Ledger:** ✅ metrics, clinical chronic-risk evidence, exposed APIs; 🟡 vendor baseline algorithms; 🔴 training data, exact recovery models.

## 19. Design Review (highlights)

- **Physician:** "HRV is a real chronic-risk marker and a weak acute one. If you imply otherwise you'll either cry wolf or miss the actual arrest. Stay in the chronic/context lane and I'll trust you."
- **Chief Scientist:** "Your baseline is only as good as your IBI cleaning and your still-window gating. Show me the artifact-correction validation before I believe any HRV number in this system."
- **Privacy advocate:** "HRV leaks mental-health and pregnancy status. This is the most inferential data you hold. Retention, on-device, no-monetization — non-negotiable, and prove it."
- **Investor:** "WHOOP monetized exactly this. Is your HRV baseline a safety input, a subscription, or both — and if both, do they share a data foundation without the wellness product weakening the safety promise?"
- **FDA reviewer:** "The moment 'HRV trend' becomes 'you have an infection,' you're a diagnostic device. Keep observations observational."

## 21. Chief Scientist's Verdict

**Confidence Ledger:** Resting HR trends as health signal — ★★★★★. Reduced HRV as *chronic* cardiac/mortality risk (ECG) — ★★★★☆. Personal-baseline-deviation > absolute thresholds — ★★★★★ (methodologically sound, universal practice). Wearable HR/HRV illness pre-detection — ★★★☆☆ (promising, multiple studies, not diagnostic). PRV = HRV under motion — ★★☆☆☆ (agree at rest, diverge in motion). HRV as an *acute* emergency trigger — ★★☆☆☆ (**weak — the key limitation gating RAXHA's HRV ambitions**). LF/HF as clean sympathovagal balance — ★★☆☆☆ (contested).
**TRL:** Resting HR — 9. Nightly HRV (RMSSD) consumer — 8–9. Personal baselining — 8 (shipped by WHOOP/Oura). Illness pre-warning — 5–6 (studies + some products, not cleared as diagnostic). Acute-event prediction from HRV — 2–3.
**Roadmap:** *MVP:* personal resting-HR/HRV baseline as infrastructure + HR-extreme corroborated triggers. *V2:* illness-pre-warning as *observation* (non-diagnostic), HRV-informed context in the risk engine, wellness-subscription surface. *Research Lab:* acute-event prediction from HRV, motion-robust HRV. *Never Build (yet):* HRV-alone acute SOS; any diagnostic illness claim without clearance; cross-user HRV comparisons.
**Competitor failures (sourced):** consumer "recovery/readiness" scores widely criticized for over-precision and day-to-day noise (documented reviewer skepticism) — a single-night HRV presented as authoritative overpromises on a noisy metric; the fix is trends + honest uncertainty. Wellness apps that implied HRV-based medical conclusions have drawn regulatory attention — the ★★-claim/★★★★★-marketing pattern again.
**Kill Criteria:** if illness-pre-warning can't beat a false-alarm/anxiety threshold in shadow mode, ship it as passive trend-visualization only (no alerts). If HRV adds no measurable lift to acute-emergency detection in validation, keep it strictly as baseline/context and say so — don't ship complexity that doesn't earn its confidence. If baseline personalization can't be done without centralizing raw IBIs, pursue federated/on-device before launch (privacy is load-bearing here).
**Historical Failures (Historian):** the broader "stress score" / "recovery score" consumer wave — several products overclaimed actionable precision from noisy single-session HRV and lost credibility with sophisticated users; the survivors (WHOOP/Oura) won by emphasizing *personal trend* over absolute number. Fetal-monitoring HRV over-intervention history (continuous monitoring raised intervention rates without always improving outcomes) — the cautionary tale that *more monitoring is not automatically better care*; a safety product must prove its alerts improve outcomes, not just multiply.
