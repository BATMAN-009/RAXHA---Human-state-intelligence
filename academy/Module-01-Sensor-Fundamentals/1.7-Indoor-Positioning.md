# Chapter 1.7 — Wi-Fi / BLE / UWB Positioning, Dead Reckoning & Indoor Positioning

> **Why this chapter closes a wound.** The emergency physician has objected in three separate chapters: *the commonest serious fall is at home, indoors, exactly where GPS fails.* Competency 6 admitted GPS is useless indoors and deferred the answer here. This is the answer — and the honest headline is that there is no single clean solution. Indoor positioning is a portfolio of imperfect techniques (Wi-Fi, BLE, UWB, inertial dead reckoning), each with a different accuracy/power/infrastructure trade-off, fused into a best-effort estimate that RAXHA must report *with its uncertainty*, never as a confident indoor pin.

---

## 1. Definition

**Indoor positioning (IPS)** estimates location where GNSS can't reach, using signals that *do* penetrate or originate indoors:
- **Wi-Fi positioning:** infer location from the set of visible access points and their signal strengths (RSSI) — either by trilateration or by matching a pre-surveyed "fingerprint."
- **Bluetooth / BLE positioning:** the same idea with BLE beacons, plus modern **direction-finding** (AoA/AoD) and **Channel Sounding** (distance by phase) for meter/sub-meter accuracy.
- **UWB (Ultra-Wideband):** time-of-flight ranging with nanosecond-scale pulses → **10–30 cm** accuracy; the precise but infrastructure-heavy option (AirTag, car keys).
- **Dead reckoning (PDR — Pedestrian Dead Reckoning):** no external signal at all — count steps (accel), estimate stride length, apply heading (gyro+mag) to propagate position from a known start. The inertial bridge through *any* signal gap.
- **Geofencing / semantic location:** often the honest best answer indoors isn't coordinates at all — it's "at home" (the registered address), which for a safety alert is frequently *enough*.

**The framing that matters for RAXHA:** outdoors you want *coordinates*; indoors you often only need *the right place, with confidence* — "at home," "left the safe zone," "in an unfamiliar building." Precision is nice; **correct semantic place + honest uncertainty is the requirement.**

## 2. History (compressed)

Wi-Fi fingerprinting research began ~2000 (Microsoft's **RADAR**, Bahl & Padmanabhan, INFOCOM 2000 — the founding IPS paper). Skyhook (2005) commercialized Wi-Fi/cell positioning, later absorbed into the Apple/Google location databases that make A-GPS and "blue dot indoors" work. iBeacon (Apple, 2013) popularized BLE proximity. **UWB** revived in consumer form with Apple's U1 chip (iPhone 11, 2019) and the FiRa/UWB standards; **BLE 5.1 (2019)** added Angle-of-Arrival direction finding; **BLE Channel Sounding (BLE 6.0, ~2024)** added phase-based ranging. **PDR** descends directly from the strapdown inertial navigation of 1950s aerospace (Chapter 1.3/1.4), scaled down to a wrist. Google/Apple both maintain massive crowd-sourced Wi-Fi/BLE location databases — the invisible infrastructure behind consumer indoor "location."

## 3. Scientific Foundation

### 3.1 The signal-to-position methods
- **RSSI trilateration:** signal strength falls with distance (roughly log-distance path loss), so RSSI → estimated range → trilaterate. **Weakness:** RSSI is brutally noisy indoors — walls, bodies, furniture, multipath, and orientation all swamp the distance signal. Accuracy: many meters, unstable.
- **Fingerprinting (the workhorse):** pre-survey a building to build a map of "at location X, you see APs {A,B,C} at strengths {…}"; at runtime, match the live scan to the closest fingerprint (kNN, or a learned model). **Strength:** absorbs the messy physics into data. **Weakness:** requires a survey, and the map goes stale when APs move or furniture changes ("fingerprint drift"). Accuracy: 2–5 m typical.
- **Time-of-flight (UWB):** measure actual signal travel time (two-way ranging) → distance directly, immune to the RSSI mess because it's timing not amplitude. UWB's huge bandwidth gives nanosecond timing → ~10 cm. **Weakness:** needs UWB anchors installed → infrastructure cost.
- **AoA/AoD (BLE 5.1+):** antenna arrays measure the *angle* a signal arrives from → bearing lines → position. Sub-meter with good hardware.

### 3.2 Pedestrian Dead Reckoning — the infrastructure-free bridge
PDR needs **no beacons**: detect steps from the accelerometer's periodic gait signature (Chapter 1.1, and deeply in the Gait competency), estimate **stride length** (a function of step frequency and height — and a major error source, since stride varies), and apply **heading** from the gyro (short-term) + magnetometer (long-term, plausibility-gated — Chapter 1.3/1.4). Position propagates from a known start. **The fatal flaw is drift:** step-count, stride, and heading errors accumulate, so PDR is excellent for seconds-to-minutes and useless for hours — *exactly the complementary profile you learned for the gyroscope,* now at the navigation scale. PDR's whole job is to bridge gaps between absolute fixes, never to replace them.

### 3.3 The unifying insight
Every indoor method is either **absolute-but-lying** (Wi-Fi/BLE RSSI: available everywhere, noisy/drifting) or **precise-but-infrastructure-bound** (UWB: accurate where anchors exist) or **relative-but-drifting** (PDR: works anywhere, unbounded error). This is the **gated-absolute-reference pattern for the third time** (magnetometer, GPS, now indoor): a drifting spine (PDR) corrected by absolute references (Wi-Fi/BLE/UWB) admitted under plausibility checks. Learn it once, apply it forever.

## 4–5. Internal Working & Hardware (brief)

Wi-Fi/BLE scanning is built into the connectivity SoC (near-free power-wise for passive scans; active scanning costs more). UWB needs a dedicated chip (Apple U1/U2, NXP, Qualcomm) *and* anchors in the environment — so it's realistic for RAXHA only where infrastructure exists (a smart-home, a care facility that installs anchors), not for arbitrary homes. PDR runs on the IMU you already have (Chapters 1.1–1.4) — zero added hardware, which is why it's RAXHA's most universally available indoor tool. Barometer (Chapter 1.16) adds **floor-level** (altitude) — critical in multi-story buildings and a cheap, underused signal.

---

## 6. Software

- **iOS:** Core Location fuses Wi-Fi/BLE/cell automatically (the "blue dot" indoors is Apple's fingerprint database at work — you rarely do it yourself); **Nearby Interaction framework** for UWB ranging (`NISession`); `CLBeacon` for iBeacon proximity; `CMPedometer` gives step/distance (a PDR primitive Apple computes for you); indoor-map venues via Apple's Indoor Maps program. Raw Wi-Fi scan lists are restricted (privacy).
- **Android:** `WifiRssi`/scan results (increasingly permission-gated and rate-limited for privacy — the same side-channel concern as high-rate motion), **`WifiRttManager`** (802.11mc Wi-Fi Round-Trip-Time → meter-level *time-of-flight* Wi-Fi ranging where APs support it), BLE scanning, UWB via the Android UWB Jetpack API, `Steps`/`StepDetector` for PDR. Fused Location blends what it can.
- **Reality:** on both platforms you mostly *consume* the OS's fused indoor estimate and its uncertainty rather than building IPS from scratch — and you supplement with your own PDR + registered-address logic for the safety case.

---

## 7. Data & 8. Algorithms

- Each estimate carries (position, accuracy, method, timestamp) — and indoor accuracy is *even less trustworthy* than GPS's, so uncertainty propagation (Decision #13) is paramount.
- **Fusion (preview of Module 8/C10):** a filter (Kalman/particle) fuses PDR (smooth, drifting) with Wi-Fi/BLE/UWB fixes (noisy/sparse, absolute) — the particle filter is especially natural indoors because it handles the multimodal "could be in room A or room C" ambiguity and map constraints (you can't walk through walls) gracefully.
- **Map-matching indoors:** floor plans constrain position (walls, doors) — powerful where a map exists (care facilities), unavailable in arbitrary homes.
- **Semantic-place classification:** often RAXHA doesn't need x,y — it needs "home / not-home / which safe-zone," which a learned classifier over Wi-Fi/BLE environment + geofence can deliver more robustly than coordinates.

## 9. Real Products

- **Apple [V]:** indoor blue-dot via Wi-Fi/BLE fingerprint DB; U1/U2 UWB (AirTag precise finding, car keys); Indoor Maps for venues; Precision Finding.
- **Google [V]:** Wi-Fi RTT (FTM), Fused Location indoor, Google Maps indoor floor plans.
- **Samsung [V]:** UWB (SmartTag+), Wi-Fi/BLE.
- **Care/enterprise IPS vendors [V]:** hospitals and assisted-living facilities deploy BLE/UWB real-time-location-systems (RTLS) for staff/patient/asset tracking — *this is the exact infrastructure that makes RAXHA's indoor-emergency case solvable in facilities* (a B2B wedge: RAXHA in a care facility with installed anchors gets precise indoor location; RAXHA in a random apartment gets "at home, ±room").
- **Life360/Tile [V]:** BLE community-find networks.

## 10. Research

RADAR (Bahl 2000, founding); Horus (probabilistic fingerprinting); UWB ToF localization literature; PDR + map-matching + Wi-Fi fusion (particle-filter indoor nav is a rich subfield); crowd-sourced/SLAM-style fingerprint construction (avoiding manual surveys). **Open problems central to RAXHA:** survey-free indoor positioning in arbitrary homes; robust floor detection; seamless indoor↔outdoor handover; and *honest uncertainty* indoors (the accuracy estimates are even more optimistic than GPS's).

## 11. Security & Privacy

Wi-Fi/BLE scanning is a **fingerprinting and tracking vector** — the set of nearby APs identifies a place and, over time, a person's movements; this is why both platforms increasingly gate Wi-Fi scan access behind location permission and rate limits. BLE beacon proximity has been abused for retail tracking. UWB is more private (short-range, secure ranging) but reveals precise presence. RAXHA's posture: consume fused OS estimates, minimize raw-scan retention, treat indoor-place data as location data (strict tier), never build a movement database (the Life360 lesson, again). Spoofing: fake beacons/APs can mislead fingerprinting → plausibility cross-checks (does the indoor estimate agree with the last GPS fix, the registered home, the inertial trail?).

## 12. Medical / Emergency Perspective

This is the chapter's whole point: **home and facility falls are the high-value emergency cases, and they're indoors.** For a responder, "at home, high confidence" is often actionable enough (they have the address). Where it isn't — a large assisted-living facility, an apartment tower — **floor and unit matter**, and that's where installed BLE/UWB RTLS earns its keep. RAXHA's honest medical claim: *reliable semantic location (which building/home) always; precise indoor location only where infrastructure supports it.* Never imply room-level precision in an arbitrary home.

## 13. Engineering for RAXHA

1. **Tiered indoor strategy, most-available first:** (a) **registered home/safe addresses + geofence** — the universal, reliable, zero-infrastructure answer ("at home"); (b) **Wi-Fi/BLE semantic place** — "home vs not-home vs which safe-zone," robust and cheap; (c) **PDR** — bridge short indoor movements from the last known fix; (d) **UWB/BLE-RTLS precise** — only where anchors exist (facilities, smart homes). Report the best tier achieved *with its confidence*.
2. **Barometer for floor level** (cheap, C1.16) in multi-story contexts.
3. **Seamless handover:** when GPS drops (entering home), fall back down the tiers without a gap; when it returns, re-acquire — the fusion filter manages continuity.
4. **Uncertainty to the responder:** "at home (high confidence)" or "near home, GPS lost 40 s ago (medium)" — never a false indoor pin.
5. **B2B facility mode:** in care facilities, integrate installed RTLS for room-level location — a distinct, higher-precision product tier (and a business wedge).
6. **Privacy:** indoor place = location data; incident-only retention; no movement database.

## 14. Failure Analysis

| Failure | Mechanism | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Confident wrong indoor pin | Noisy RSSI / stale fingerprint | Report semantic place + confidence, not coordinates; corroborate |
| PDR drifts far from truth | Accumulated step/stride/heading error | Bound PDR horizon; correct with any absolute fix; never long-run PDR alone |
| Wrong floor | No altitude source | Barometer floor detection; facility maps |
| Fingerprint stale | APs/furniture moved | Crowd-update; degrade to semantic place; confidence down |
| Fake-beacon spoofing | Unauthenticated Wi-Fi/BLE | Cross-check GPS-last/registered-home/inertial trail |
| No infrastructure in a real home | UWB/RTLS unavailable | Fall back to "at home" semantic — honest and usually sufficient |
| Privacy leak via scans | Scan retention / movement inference | Minimize retention; consume fused estimate; strict location tier |

## 15. RAXHA Application — distilled

- **Use as:** the indoor answer GPS can't give — primarily as **reliable semantic place** ("at home / left safe-zone") with honest confidence, escalating to precise location only where infrastructure exists.
- **Do NOT use as:** a source of confident room-level coordinates in an arbitrary home; a long-run PDR-only position; a movement-tracking database.
- **One-liner:** *outdoors RAXHA needs coordinates; indoors it needs the right place with honest confidence — and at home, "at home" is usually the right answer.*

## 16. Future

Survey-free IPS (SLAM-style crowd-built maps); ubiquitous Wi-Fi RTT and BLE Channel Sounding pushing consumer indoor to sub-meter without dedicated anchors; UWB proliferation; tight visual-inertial-radio fusion; standardized emergency indoor location (regulatory push for E911 vertical/floor accuracy — directly relevant to RAXHA's mission).

## 18. Founder Intelligence

**Why hasn't Apple solved indoor perfectly?** Because there's no universal indoor infrastructure — Wi-Fi/BLE fingerprints need surveys or crowd data, UWB needs anchors, PDR drifts. Apple brute-forces it with a massive crowd-sourced database + UWB where it controls the ecosystem; still no room-level in an arbitrary home. **WHOOP/Oura:** no location play — out of scope for their body-focused model. **Garmin:** outdoor-first; indoor is a non-goal. **Google:** owns the Wi-Fi/fingerprint database + Maps indoor + Wi-Fi RTT standard — platform leverage. **Enterprise RTLS vendors** (Stanley/AeroScout, CenTrak, etc.): a whole industry selling BLE/UWB indoor location to hospitals — proof the *facility* market pays for indoor precision. **Why doesn't everyone do indoor safety?** No universal solution, survey/infrastructure cost, privacy sensitivity of indoor tracking, and honest uncertainty is hard to sell. **Startup opportunities:** survey-free home IPS; care-facility fall-location (RAXHA + RTLS integration — a concrete B2B wedge); indoor emergency location for first responders (a regulatory tailwind exists); floor-level detection as a service. **RAXHA strategy:** win the *common* case (home) with the *cheapest reliable* tool (registered address + semantic place), and offer a *premium facility tier* (RTLS integration) where precision is both needed and infrastructure-funded. Do NOT over-invest in arbitrary-home room-level precision — it's unsolved, and "at home, high confidence" already saves the life. **PhD gaps:** survey-free robust home IPS; honest indoor uncertainty; seamless indoor-outdoor; privacy-preserving indoor location. **Patents:** Wi-Fi fingerprinting (Microsoft RADAR lineage, Skyhook), UWB ranging (Apple/NXP/Qualcomm — query `ultra-wideband ranging positioning assignee:X`), BLE AoA/RTT, RTLS families. **Ledger:** ✅ methods, standards (Wi-Fi RTT, BLE 5.1/6.0, UWB), OS APIs, RTLS market; 🟡 crowd-DB internals; 🔴 vendor fingerprint algorithms.

**Confidence Ledger:** UWB ToF ~10–30 cm where anchored — ★★★★★. Wi-Fi/BLE fingerprinting 2–5 m — ★★★☆☆ (works, survey/drift-limited). RSSI trilateration — ★★☆☆☆ (noisy). PDR over short horizons — ★★★★☆; PDR standalone over long horizons — ★★☆☆☆ (drift). "Registered home + geofence = reliable 'at home'" — ★★★★★. Room-level location in an *arbitrary* home — ★★☆☆☆ (**largely unsolved without infrastructure — the honest limit**). Facility RTLS room-level — ★★★★☆ (deployed in industry).
**TRL:** Wi-Fi/BLE fused indoor (OS) — 8–9. UWB ranging — 8–9 (where anchored). Wi-Fi RTT — 7–8 (AP-dependent). PDR — 8. Survey-free arbitrary-home IPS — 3–4. Facility RTLS fall-location integration — 6–7 (industry-proven RTLS; RAXHA integration to build).
**Roadmap:** *MVP:* registered-address + geofence semantic place + PDR bridge + barometer floor hint. *V2:* Wi-Fi/BLE semantic-place classifier, indoor-outdoor handover, facility RTLS tier (B2B). *Research:* survey-free home IPS, honest indoor uncertainty. *Never Build:* confident room-level pins in arbitrary homes; indoor movement databases; UWB dependence for consumers without anchors.
