# Competency 9 (Science) — Human Activity Recognition (HAR)

> **Why HAR sits here.** Gait (Competency 8) gave you the locomotion primitive — walking, its speed, its variability. HAR is the full **behavioral vocabulary** built on top of it: walking, running, sitting, standing, lying, climbing stairs, cycling, driving, sleeping, eating, and — the one RAXHA exists for — *falling*. HAR is the layer that turns a stream of accelerations into a *label a human understands*, and it is the direct substrate of RAXHA's context engine: you cannot gate a fall alarm on "the user was doing vigorous exercise" until you can *recognize* vigorous exercise. This chapter also carries Doctrine #16 into practice — the best HAR is personalized (your "sitting" is not mine).

---

## 1. Definition

**Human Activity Recognition** is the automatic classification of a person's physical activity from sensor data — mapping a window of raw signals (accelerometer, gyroscope, sometimes HR, location, barometer) to an activity label. It is a **time-series classification** problem with a fixed, human-meaningful output vocabulary.

Two kinds of output, both matter for RAXHA:
- **Ambulation/posture states:** walking, running, standing, sitting, lying, stairs, cycling, driving, still — the *context* states that gate detection (a "fall" while lying in bed is suspect; a hard "impact" while cycling is likely a bump).
- **Events:** fall, near-fall — rare, transient, safety-critical, and the reason RAXHA treats HAR not as a fitness feature but as a safety substrate.

The subtlety that defines the field: activities are **hierarchical and context-dependent** (walking-while-carrying-groceries, sitting-in-a-moving-car), **person-specific** (gait, style, pace vary), and **imbalanced** (falls are ~0% of data — the base-rate problem from Competency 1, now as a *training* problem).

## 2. History

- **1999–2004:** wearable HAR emerges; **Bao & Intille (2004)** "Activity recognition from user-annotated acceleration data" (*Pervasive*) — multi-accelerometer, classical features + decision-tree/kNN; the founding wearable-HAR paper and the citation ancestor of everything here.
- **2004–2014 (classical era):** hand-crafted features (time + frequency domain) + Random Forest / SVM / HMM. Benchmark datasets appear: **UCI-HAR, WISDM, PAMAP2, Opportunity**. Interpretable, data-efficient, still the right choice when data/compute are scarce.
- **2014→ (deep era):** CNNs learn features from raw windows; **Ordóñez & Roggen (2016) DeepConvLSTM** — conv layers for spatial feature extraction + LSTM for temporal context — becomes the default deep HAR baseline.
- **2019→ (self-supervised era):** labeled wearable data is scarce and falls are rare, so **self-supervised pretraining** on massive unlabeled data + fine-tuning (Saeed et al. 2019 multi-task SSL; Haresamudram, Tang, and others; **Yuan et al. 2024** UK-Biobank-scale IMU pretraining, *npj Digital Medicine*) becomes the frontier — the "foundation model for motion" direction, directly relevant to RAXHA's scarce-label reality.
- **Now:** transformers for HAR; on-device/TinyML HAR (the B8 pairing); personalization and domain adaptation as active problems.

## 3. Scientific Foundation

### 3.1 The pipeline (memorize; it's the spine of the whole field)
```
raw sensor stream
   → windowing (segment into fixed windows, e.g. 2–5 s, 50% overlap)
   → preprocessing (filter, gravity-separate, resample to uniform grid — B5)
   → feature extraction (hand-crafted OR learned)
   → classification (per-window label)
   → temporal smoothing (HMM / voting — activities persist; don't flicker)
```

### 3.2 Windowing — the most underrated decision
Activities have characteristic timescales: a step is ~1 s, a fall is ~1–2 s, "sitting" is minutes. **Window length is a bias-variance knob:** too short and you can't see a full gait cycle or a complete fall; too long and you blur transitions and delay detection (a safety-latency cost). Overlap trades compute for temporal resolution. For RAXHA, the fall path wants short, low-latency windows with a pre-event buffer (Competency 1's ring buffer); the context path can use longer windows.

### 3.3 Features
- **Time-domain:** mean, variance, RMS, signal-magnitude-area (SMA), zero/mean crossings, correlation between axes, percentiles, peak counts.
- **Frequency-domain:** FFT band energies, spectral entropy, dominant frequency (walking ~1.5–2.5 Hz; running higher) — the same spectral machinery as PPG cadence (Ch 1.8) and gait (C8).
- **Orientation-invariant features** (magnitude, from Ch 1.1) matter because the watch sits at arbitrary wrist angles.
- **Learned features:** CNNs discover these automatically from raw windows — better when data is plentiful, less interpretable, hungrier.

### 3.4 Why falls break the standard pipeline (the RAXHA-critical part)
Standard HAR assumes **balanced, recurring, sustained** activities. Falls are **rare, one-shot, transient, and safety-critical** — so:
- **Class imbalance:** a fall is ~0.001% of data → a classifier that always says "not fall" is 99.999% accurate and useless (the base-rate trap as a *training* pathology). Fixes: resampling, cost-sensitive loss, anomaly-detection framing (Module 9), synthetic/simulated falls (with the SisFall→FARSEEING domain-gap caveat, Ch 1.1).
- **Transience:** a fall is one event, not a repeated pattern — you can't average many cycles like you can for walking.
- **Consequence asymmetry:** a missed fall ≫ a false fall in cost, but too many false falls ⇒ disabled product (alarm fatigue). The whole cascade (Ch 1.1 §8) exists to resolve this.
- **Therefore falls are usually a *separate* pipeline** (trigger + confirm cascade), with general HAR providing the *context* that gates it — not one big classifier that does both.

## 4–5. Internal Working & "Hardware" (where HAR runs)
HAR runs across the tiers (B1): simple activity/step detection on the **sensor hub / ML core** (µA, always-on — ST MLC, Bosch smart-hub run HAR *inside the IMU*); windowed TinyML classifiers on the **watch MCU** (Tier 1, B8); richer context models on the **phone** (Tier 2, fusing location/HR). The power gradient is the same as always: cheap sensors and cheap models always-on, expensive models gated by them.

## 6. Software
- **iOS:** Core Motion **`CMMotionActivityManager`** gives OS-level activity classification (stationary, walking, running, automotive, cycling — with confidence) *for free and at near-zero power* — RAXHA should **consume this for context gating** rather than reinventing it; plus workout types via HealthKit.
- **Android/Wear:** **Activity Recognition API** (`ActivityRecognitionClient`, `DetectedActivity` with confidences) and Health Services exercise detection — same "consume the platform's HAR for context" logic. `ACTIVITY_RECOGNITION` runtime permission required.
- **The reuse lesson (Decision from B-track):** platforms already ship decent *context* HAR; RAXHA's differentiated ML effort goes into the *safety events* (fall/crash) and *personalized baselines* the platforms don't own — not into re-detecting "walking."

## 7. Data
- Windowed, labeled activity segments; quality/staleness carried (B5); heavy class imbalance for events.
- **Labeling is the bottleneck:** real-world labels are expensive and falls are almost unobtainable in the wild (FARSEEING is precious because real elderly falls are rare) → self-supervised pretraining + simulated falls + the fleet's own shadow-mode labels (every user-canceled alert = a labeled false positive; Ch B1 §7). RAXHA's operation *is* its labeling engine.

## 8. Algorithms
- **Classical:** RF/SVM/kNN on hand-crafted features + HMM smoothing — interpretable, data-efficient, TinyML-friendly (often the right MVP choice).
- **Deep:** CNN (learned features) → DeepConvLSTM (temporal) → Transformers (long-range, attention). Better with data; heavier.
- **Self-supervised / foundation:** pretrain on unlabeled population IMU, fine-tune per task/person — the scarce-label answer.
- **Personalization (Doctrine #16 in practice):** a global model initializes; per-user adaptation (fine-tuning, or personal-baseline residuals) governs — "your walking" calibrates the model. Federated learning (Module 13) personalizes without centralizing raw motion.
- **Temporal smoothing:** activities persist; a single-window "fall" flanked by "walking" is likely noise — HMM/voting/debounce (the hysteresis lesson, again).

## 9. Real Products
Apple activity/workout detection + fall/crash (context HAR + safety events, ✅); Google Activity Recognition API + Fitbit exercise auto-detect; Garmin activity profiles; every fitness wearable auto-classifies workouts. The pattern: **context HAR is commoditized and platform-provided; safety-event detection and personalization are where differentiation lives** — exactly RAXHA's wedge.

## 10. Research
Bao & Intille 2004; DeepConvLSTM 2016; self-supervised IMU (Saeed 2019, Yuan 2024); the HAR benchmark suite (UCI-HAR/WISDM/PAMAP2/Opportunity); fall-specific datasets (SisFall/MobiAct simulated; FARSEEING real). **Open problems:** real-world label scarcity, domain shift (lab→life, person→person, wrist-placement), personalization without labels, and calibrated *uncertainty* on activity labels (a low-confidence "driving" should gate differently than a confident one — Decision #13).

## 11. Security & Privacy
Activity labels are behavioral/inferential (reveal routine, exercise, driving, sleep) → sensitive; on-device processing, minimization. Motion data is also identity-bearing (gait, Ch 1.1 §11 / C8) → HAR pipelines touch biometric data. Adversarial examples can fool HAR classifiers (a concern if an attacker wants to suppress a fall label) → cross-sensor corroboration and anomaly framing as defense.

## 12. Medical Perspective
HAR enables the *context* that makes medical detection trustworthy (distinguishing exercise tachycardia from pathological; a fall from sitting down). Activity/mobility levels are themselves health signals (sedentary behavior, activity decline — overlaps C8/Module 10). Screening-not-diagnosis holds. The safety-critical medical requirement: **context HAR must be right when it gates a life-critical decision** — a wrongly-labeled "driving" that suppresses a real fall alarm is a lethal failure (Decision #13: low-confidence context should *widen* the alarm, not silence it).

## 13. Engineering for RAXHA
1. **Consume platform context HAR** (Core Motion activity, Android Activity Recognition) for gating — don't rebuild "walking/driving/still."
2. **Own the safety-event pipeline** (fall/crash) as a *separate* cascade (trigger→confirm), with context HAR as an input that *gates* but never *silences* (Decision #13).
3. **Personalize** (Doctrine #16): global model initializes, per-user baseline governs; federated updates (Module 13).
4. **Window discipline:** short low-latency windows + pre-event buffer for the fall path; longer windows for context.
5. **Temporal smoothing** on context labels (don't let a one-window glitch flip escalation).
6. **Shadow mode is the label engine:** every canceled alert and confirmed event is training data; the fleet teaches the model (Ch B1 §7).
7. **TinyML on-watch** for the always-on confirmer (B8), phone for rich context fusion.

## 14. Failure Analysis
| Failure | Mechanism | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| "Walking" reported as "driving" | Wrist motion ambiguity; poor model | Confidence-aware gating; corroborate (GPS speed); widen-not-silence on low confidence |
| Missed fall in training | Class imbalance | Cost-sensitive/anomaly framing; simulated + shadow data |
| Overfits lab, fails in life | Domain shift (SisFall→FARSEEING) | Real-world validation; personalization; SSL pretraining |
| One-window false "fall" flips alarm | No temporal smoothing | HMM/voting/debounce + confirm cascade |
| Context wrongly silences real fall | Over-trusted low-confidence label | Decision #13: low-confidence context widens the alarm |
| Model too big for watch | Deep model, tiny MCU | TinyML quantization/pruning (B8); tiered inference |
| Person-specific miss | Global model, atypical gait | Personalization (Doctrine #16); per-user adaptation |

## 15. RAXHA Application — distilled
- **Use as:** the *context engine* (platform-provided activity states that gate detection) + the *safety-event substrate* (own fall/crash cascade). HAR is what lets RAXHA say "impact + but the user is cycling → downweight" or "impact + user was walking → then went still and horizontal → escalate."
- **Do NOT use as:** a single monolithic classifier doing both context and falls; a context gate that *silences* rather than *downweights*; a global model where a personal one belongs.
- **One-liner:** *HAR is how RAXHA knows what the person was doing, so it can tell whether what just happened is normal for that activity or an emergency.*

## 16. Future
Foundation models for motion (pretrain-once, fine-tune-per-person); on-device continual/online learning; calibrated-uncertainty HAR; multimodal HAR (IMU+audio+location+physiology); the convergence of context HAR, gait, and anomaly detection into one personalized human-state model (Modules 8–10).

## 18. Founder Intelligence
**Why hasn't the problem been "solved"?** Context HAR is largely solved and commoditized (every phone does it); *safety-event* HAR in the real world (soft falls, elderly, wrist placement) is NOT — it's the same open problem from Ch 1.1, now with better tools but the same label scarcity. **WHOOP/Oura/Garmin/Fitbit/Apple/Google** all ship activity classification as table-stakes; none of them owns *cross-platform personalized safety* HAR. **Why doesn't everyone build fall HAR well?** Labels (real falls are unobtainable), domain shift, personalization difficulty, and the base-rate/alarm-fatigue trap. **Startup opportunities:** the label/data moat (whoever accumulates real-world fall data wins — RAXHA's shadow mode is this engine), personalization infrastructure, foundation-models-for-motion as licensable IP, elder-specific HAR. **RAXHA strategy:** consume commodity context HAR, pour differentiated ML into safety events + personalization + the fleet-data flywheel (shadow mode → labels → better models → more trust → more users → more data). This flywheel *is* the compounding moat; detection algorithms are copyable, but a validated real-world fall-data asset is not. **PhD gaps:** real-world fall HAR, label-free personalization, calibrated activity uncertainty, wrist-placement robustness. **Patents:** activity-recognition families (Apple/Google — query `activity recognition accelerometer assignee:X`); fall-detection HAR; SSL-for-wearables (emerging). **Ledger:** ✅ platform HAR APIs, benchmark datasets, published architectures; 🟡 vendor production models; 🔴 training data, real-world fall corpora (the scarce asset).

## 19. Design Review (highlights)
- **Chief Scientist:** "Show me you validated on *real-world* falls, not SisFall. Your lab number is meaningless if it doesn't transfer to FARSEEING-class data."
- **Physician:** "Context gating that *silences* a real fall because it mislabeled 'driving' will kill someone. Prove low-confidence context widens, never silences."
- **Investor:** "Everyone has activity recognition. Your moat is the real-world fall-data flywheel from shadow mode. How fast does it compound, and is it defensible?"
- **Privacy advocate:** "HAR touches gait and routine — identity-bearing. On-device, personalized, federated. No behavioral-profiling API."
- **ML reviewer:** "Class imbalance will make your accuracy number a lie. Report per-class recall and false-alarms-per-person-week, not accuracy."

## 21. Chief Scientist's Verdict
**Confidence Ledger:** Context HAR (walk/run/still/drive) — ★★★★★ (commoditized). Real-world soft-fall HAR at the wrist — ★★☆☆☆ (open problem). Personalized HAR > global — ★★★★☆. SSL pretraining for label scarcity — ★★★★☆ (strong, emerging). Simulated falls transferring to real elderly falls — ★★☆☆☆ (domain gap). Calibrated activity uncertainty — ★★★☆☆.
**TRL:** Context activity recognition — 9. Fall-event HAR (impact) — 7–8; (soft/real-world) — 3–4. On-device TinyML HAR — 8. Personalized/federated HAR — 5–6. Foundation-model HAR — 4–5.
**Roadmap:** *MVP:* consume platform context HAR; own impact-fall cascade; report per-class metrics. *V2:* personalization, shadow-mode retraining flywheel, SSL pretraining. *Research:* real-world soft-fall HAR, foundation models, uncertainty calibration. *Never Build:* one monolithic context+fall classifier; context that silences falls; accuracy-as-headline-metric; global-only models for a personal problem.
**Competitor failures (sourced):** the documented gap between wearable HAR lab benchmarks and real-world performance (a recurring theme in the literature) — labs overfit; fielded systems disappoint. Fitness trackers' documented activity-misclassification complaints (driving logged as steps, etc.) — context HAR is "good enough" for fitness, not for safety-gating without corroboration. The fall-detection field's SisFall→FARSEEING generalization gap (Ch 1.1) — the canonical HAR-for-safety honesty check.
**Kill Criteria:** if real-world fall HAR can't beat the platform's own fall detection in shadow mode, ride the platform and compete on response/personalization (the B2 kill criterion, restated). If context HAR confidence can't be calibrated well enough to gate safely, gate conservatively (widen alarms) and accept more false positives over missed falls. If personalization needs centralized raw motion, do federated first.
**Historical Failures (Historian):** the wearable-HAR "solved in the lab, broken in life" pattern (well-documented) — the reason real-world validation is a doctrine, not a nicety. Google's activity-recognition over-reach in early fitness apps (misclassifications eroding trust). The broader ML-in-production lesson: imbalanced-class problems where accuracy hid catastrophic per-class failure (the metric-choice trap that has sunk many ML products).
